1. Core Concept — what a starter is

A sourdough starter is a live colony of wild yeast and bacteria living in flour and water. It's the entire engine behind the bread — no commercial yeast needed.

Root idea: the yeast makes gas (the rise), the bacteria makes acid (the tang). Feed them fresh flour and they thrive and rise; starve them and they go sour and sluggish. Every rule below is just a consequence of that one fact.

2. Feeding — the 1:1:1 ratio

"Feeding" = discard most of the old starter, then give what's left equal weights of fresh flour and water. The colony gets a fresh food supply and wakes back up.

ComponentAmountNotes
starter25 gkeep this much, discard the rest
flour25 gplain / all-purpose / bread flour
water25 glukewarm; filtered if your tap is chlorinated
# one feed, by weight — equal parts $ feed --starter 25g --flour 25g --water 25g → stir to a thick paste, loose lid, wait for rise
The discard: every feed you keep a spoonful and toss the rest — normal, not wasteful. Stash discard in a jar in the fridge and use it for pancakes, waffles, or crackers once you've got enough.
Never seal the lid. Rest the lid on top or leave it loose — do not screw it down airtight. A feeding starter gives off CO₂, and a sealed jar builds pressure until it pops the lid, cracks the glass, or makes a mess on the counter. She needs to breathe.

3. Maintenance — pick one mode

COUNTER

  • Feed every 12–24 hrs
  • Room temp, lid resting on (not sealed)
  • Doubles then falls — that peak is your leavening
  • Best if you bake a couple times a week

FRIDGE

  • Feed once a week
  • Feed, sit out 1–2 hrs, then chill
  • Cold = slow = low maintenance
  • To bake: pull out, feed 1–2× over a day to wake it

Rule of thumb: baking a lot → counter. Baking now and then → fridge. You can switch anytime.

4. Readiness — the float test

A few hours after feeding, drop a small spoonful of starter into a glass of water.

PASS: it floats → full of gas, at peak, ready to bake.
FAIL: it sinks → not ready. Give it more time or another feed.

At peak she's bubbly, roughly doubled, domed on top, and smells pleasantly sour and yeasty — think tangy beer, not nail polish.

PASS signals

  • doubled in volume since feeding
  • surface full of bubbles, domed not sunken
  • floats in water
  • smells tangy / yeasty, not sharp like acetone

5. The Loaf — classic Dutch-oven boule

One loaf · ~24 hrs mostly hands-off · needs a heavy lidded pot.

Ingredients

ItemWeight
bread flour500 g
water (warm)350 g
active starter100 g
salt10 g
Get a scale. Sourdough is all ratios — cups will betray you. A $12 kitchen scale is the best money you'll spend on this.

Method

  1. Prep the night before. Feed the starter so it's bubbly and passes the float test when you begin.
  2. Mix~10 min
    Combine flour + water into a shaggy dough, rest 30–60 min (autolyse). Then add starter + salt and squish together until cohesive.
  3. Stretch & fold2–3 hrs
    Every 30 min: wet hand, lift one side, stretch up, fold over. Rotate ×4. Do 3–4 rounds total, then rest. Goes from sticky lump to smooth and puffy.
  4. Bulk rise4–8 hrs
    Cover, room temp, until grown ~50% and jiggly/bubbly. Warm kitchen = faster.
  5. Shape~10 min
    Tip out, fold into a tight round, rest 15 min, shape tight again. Place seam-up in a floured towel-lined bowl.
  6. Cold proof12–16 hrs
    Cover, fridge overnight. Builds flavor and makes scoring easy.
  7. Preheat45 min
    Heat the empty Dutch oven (lid on) to 500°F / 260°C. Once it's up to temp, drop the oven to 450°F / 230°C right before the loaf goes in.
  8. Bake50 min total
    Flip dough onto parchment, slash the top ~½" deep. Lower into the hot pot.
# the bake — Jarnisha house timing $ preheat --temp 500F # then drop to 450F as the loaf goes in $ bake --lid on --temp 450F --time 30m → trapped steam gives the big oven-spring rise $ bake --lid off --temp 450F --time 20m → crust browns and crisps → pull at deep golden-brown
Then wait. Cool on a rack at least 1 hr before cutting — it's still cooking inside and slicing hot ruins the crumb. Hardest step of the whole thing.

Bake-day checklist

  • starter fed & floating before you start
  • everything weighed on a scale
  • dough ~50% risen before shaping (don't underproof)
  • pot preheated fully before dough goes in
  • cooled 1 hr+ before slicing
